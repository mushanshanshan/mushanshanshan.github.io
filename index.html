1. Abstract

With the widespread use of speech synthesis technology in recent years, an increasing number of circumstances have necessitated zero-shot speaker adaptive text-to-speech (TTS) and voice conversion (VC), that is, synthesizing speech for new voice based on seconds of reference speech samples, or converting speech from one unseen speaker to another unseen speaker. Existing zero-shot speaker adaptive TTS and VC approaches suffer from inadequate model generalization in out-of-distribution data, resulting in poor synthesized voice quality for unseen speakers. In this paper, we propose RSA-TV, a robust zero-shot speaker adaptive TTS and VC model that can achieve promising performance on unseen speakers. Specifically, we leverage the representation learning capabilities of the variational autoencoder and introduce adversarial training for feature disentanglement to improve model robustness. In addition, we propose reverse speaker similarity loss to improve the speaker similarity of synthesized speech further. Our experiments demonstrate that RSA-TV outperforms all baseline models in both zero-shot TTS and VC tasks.

5. Conclusion

In this paper, we proposed RSA-TV, a robust zero-shot speaker adaptive TTS and VC model. In particular, we leverage the feature extraction capacity of the variational autoencoder for improving speaker feature extraction and introduce adversarial training for text and speaker feature disentanglement. In addition, we propose a novel reverse speaker representation loss for Vits. Experiment results demonstrate that RSA-TV achieves signicantly improved adaptation performance in the zero-shot scenario.
